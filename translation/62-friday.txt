All that which is in the Heavens and all that which is on the Earth glorifies God— the King, the Holy, the Almighty, the Wise.
It is He who has raised up amidst the Gentiles a messenger from among them; he recites His revelations to them, purifies them, and teaches the Scripture and Wisdom to them—although they were previously in clear error—
and to the others who have yet to join them. And He is the Almighty, the Wise.
That is the favor of God— He gives it to whomever He wills, for God is the possessor of great favor.
The parable for those who had been tasked to carry the Torah but have not carried it out is like a donkey that carries books. How wretched a parable for a people who reject God’s revelations! And God does not guide the people who do wrong.
Say: O you all who practice Judaism! If you claim that you are the closest to God to the exclusion of humanity, then long for death, should you be truthful.
But they would never long for it due to what their hands have brought about. And God is cognizant of the wrongdoers.
Say: The death from which you flee— it will surely meet you. You will then be returned to the Knowledgeable of the Unseen and the Seen, and He will inform you of that which you used to do.
O you all who believe! When you are called to prayer on the day of Friday, proceed to the remembrance of God and leave business behind; that is best for you, if only you knew.
Then, once the prayer has concluded, disperse throughout the land, seek God’s bounty, and remember God often, so that you may succeed.
But when they saw a bargain or distraction [during your sermon, O prophet], they scattered towards it and left you standing there. Say: That which is with God is better than any distraction or bargain! And God is the best of providers.
